\section{Motivation}

\begin{frame}[t]{Reading the brain, one neuron at a time}
\begin{columns}[T]
% ---- Left: text ----------------------------------------------------------
\begin{column}{0.54\textwidth}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Calcium imaging} records brain activity: when a neuron fires,
      intracellular $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ rises and a fluorescent indicator
      brightens --- \textbf{active neurons \enquote{light up} over time}
      (a 2D${}+{}$t video).
\item To turn this video into science, each neuron's
      \textbf{soma must be segmented}, then its \textbf{activity trace}
      ($\Delta F/F$ over time) can be read out.
\item \textbf{Manual annotation is slow, subjective, and does not scale}
      --- it is the real bottleneck.
\item \textbf{Our angle:} raw video is abundant and cheap; labels are scarce
      and expensive. Can \textbf{self-supervision on unlabeled video} cut how
      many labels we need?
\end{itemize}
\end{column}
